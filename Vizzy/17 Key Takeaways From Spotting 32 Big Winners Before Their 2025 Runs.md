## 1- High ADR%/ATR%

On average, these winners showed an ADR% of 7.31% (median 6.91%) and an ATR% of 8.34% (median 7.58%).
Across the list, the lowest ADR% at the time of addition was 3.31% and 3.50% for the ATR%. Those were the exceptions, not the rule.
High ADR% and ATR% are critical when looking for stocks with a realistic chance of delivering 100%+ moves in a relatively short period of time. 
There is a clear correlation between performance and volatility: the higher the ADR% and ATR%, the greater the potential for outsized moves.
On top of that, time is money. 
Capital tied up in slow-moving stocks is capital that can’t be redeployed. If your goal is high performance, you need to be able to recycle your edge repeatedly. Being stuck in low-volatility names is a recipe for average returns at best.
For that reason, the sweet spot is stocks that exhibit both:
	•	High ADR%, which helps build a cushion quickly, and
	•	High ATR%, which increases the odds of a sustained multi-day or multi-week move.

## 2- Basket Momentum

Basket momentum is a critical component. I use the term basket because I group stocks into custom themes or industries using my own tools. Stocks rarely move in a vacuum — they tend to move as part of a group. When an entire basket is gaining momentum, the odds of individual stocks delivering sustained moves increase dramatically.
Every single one of these big winners began the major part of their advances while the basket they belonged had strong upward momentum.
To measure that, I use a proprietary mix of advance/decline data and moving averages to gauge momentum across each basket I created.
Trading becomes significantly easier when you’re aligned with basket momentum. When the broader theme is pushing higher, individual setups have a much higher chance of working — it’s simply a case of having the wind at your back.

## 3- Relative Strength

Almost every single big winner also stood out as one of the strongest stocks within its own group, consistently outperforming its peers before and during the move. 
On top of that, these stocks didn’t just lead their sectors; they also showed clear and persistent outperformance versus other sectors and the broader market. 
Focusing on relative strength helps keep you aligned with where money is actually flowing, rather than where you think it should flow — and time and again, it proves to be a defining characteristic of the biggest winners.
 Short-term moves can be driven by noise, positioning, or one-off headlines. What matters is persistence: relative strength that holds up over days and weeks, especially through market pullbacks and rotations.

## 4- Clean Chart

Despite being high ADR% and ATR% stocks, these big winners consistently displayed orderly daily charts before and during their advances. There was visible tightness, well-defined consolidations, and controlled pullbacks that respected key levels and moving averages. Price tended to progress in staircase-like patterns rather than chaotic swings. 
Clean structure makes trends more sustainable, risk easier to define, and entries far more repeatable. 
In contrast, charts that are “all over the place” reflect instability and randomness - conditions that may produce sharp moves, but rarely lead to durable, high-quality trends.

## 5- Moving Average Squeeze

This really stood out to me when I went through all of these winners. 
Almost all of them experienced a clear moving-average squeeze, where the 10-, 20-, and 50-day moving averages became tightly clustered before the move began. 
That compression reflects a period of balance, of tight daily price action — volatility contracts, supply and demand come into alignment, and weak hands are gradually shaken out. When those same moving averages then start to turn up and expand, it signals that momentum is resolving in one direction and that a sustained trend is likely underway. 
This setup offers a powerful edge because risk can be clearly defined during the compression phase, while the subsequent expansion often marks the beginning of powerful and sustained moves.

## 6- Catalyst

One recurring trait across all 32 stocks was the presence of a clear catalyst. Large, sustained moves rarely happen in a vacuum. In most cases, the trigger was identifiable — earnings surprises, industry-wide developments, analyst upgrades, new contracts, or the launch of new products or services. These catalysts don’t just move individual stocks; they often attract institutional attention and unlock fresh demand. Importantly, the goal isn’t to predict news, but to recognize when a stock is positioned to respond aggressively once new information hits. When the setup is right, a catalyst acts as the spark that ignites a much larger move.

## 7- Consolidation phase

Equally important was structure. Every single one of these winners broke out of a well-defined weekly consolidation before their advance. These bases lasted anywhere from 5 weeks to as long as 55 weeks, with an average consolidation period of 13 weeks. That time spent digesting prior gains allowed weak hands to exit, supply to dry up, and volatility to compress — setting the stage for powerful expansions once price resolved higher. Long, orderly consolidations aren’t a sign of stagnation; they are often the preparation phase for the next major leg. When a stock emerges from such a base with volume and momentum, the odds of a sustained trend increase drastically.

## 8- Group Setup

The best opportunities happen when several stocks are setting up and breaking out at the same time in a group. That's what happened in 2025 to Quantum Computing, Rare Earth, Fintech, Space, Crypto Mining, Data Centers, ... And the same goes at a higher level. Pay close attention when not only stocks within a basket, but multiple baskets set up and break out at the same time or even one after the other in a short period of time. That kind of alignment often signals a broader rotation and is when it pays off to position for larger, more sustained moves.

## 9- Big Moves Require Patience

Big moves don’t happen overnight. Even with high ADR%/ATR% stocks, expecting a 100%+ trade to materialize in a few days is usually unrealistic. The most meaningful moves need time to develop, often progressing through periods of frustration and pullbacks. Patience is required to let a stock work in your favor, and discipline is essential to stay with it long enough for the thesis to play out. The real challenge isn’t just finding the right stock - it’s resisting the urge to sell too early and holding the position until there is a clear, valid reason to exit.

## 10- Focus on the leaders

“Focus on the leaders.” I’ve heard that phrase thousands of times throughout my trading career, and for a long time it felt vague and unhelpful. Everyone talks about leaders, but few clearly define what that actually means. It wasn’t until I developed my own, rules-based definition that the concept truly clicked.

A leader, in my book, is simple and objective: a high ADR% / ATR% stock that shows some of the strongest relative strength within a strong basket. That’s it — no stories, no narratives, no guessing. These are the stocks where capital is clearly flowing, volatility is sufficient to produce meaningful moves, and price is already outperforming peers.

Focusing on leaders matters because leadership tends to persist. The strongest stocks in the strongest groups are usually the first to move, the ones that move the furthest, and the last to break down when conditions deteriorate. By concentrating on these names, you stack multiple edges at once: volatility, relative strength, and group momentum.
